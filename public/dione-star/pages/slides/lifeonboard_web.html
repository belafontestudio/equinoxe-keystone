— Life On Board —

Onboard Dione Star we look forward to help you relax and enjoy your time with us with a close and natural contact with the sea. You will find that swimming and water sports are made particularly easy, with a large shaded floating platform, 2 jet skis, scooba diving equipment, Sea Doo Acquascooters and easy steps way down into the water. Tenders are equipped with showers and fridges to allow you long days out at sea.

On Deck Intro

On deck, guests can enjoy walking bare-foot along the smooth teak decks, or lounging in the ample social seating, either under the sun or in the shade. They can sip a sundowner whilst relaxing on swath of deep cushions on the upper deck, or dine al fresco beneath the stars.

Down Below

Down below, there are 6 cabins to choose from, each accurately finished by Italian craftsmen in a classic yet informal style, and with full en-suite facilities. The entertainment system includes a large TV in every cabin, along with WiFi and stereo, with access to an extensive on board film library.

On Sail

When under sail, Dione Star handles beautifully, mainly thanks to her deep keel and easy motion. When at anchor, there is a fleet of fast tenders, jet skis and paddle-boards at your disposal, along with ample fishing, scuba and water-skiing equipment.
As a base for any water sports, you could drift on the floating platform, fully sheltered by a sun awning, dangling your toes in the water while enjoying a lazy brunch.

Cabins

Dione Star can accommodate 12 guests in 6 cabins: a combination of Italian design and simple refinement, happily recreates a mixture of the maritime feel of an old sailing boat together with the looks of a cozy childhood holiday home. The finishes vary between the cabins – some are trimmed in mahogany, others in natural bamboo, and the rest in clarified oak, but all maintain the style of a true classic. There is also a large opening porthole, giving guests a fresh sea breeze and closer contact with their surroundings.

Resting

Most of the sleeping accommodation is on the lower deck, and includes an owners cabin, and four guest cabins. Two of the guest cabins have a pair of single beds, but the rest are all doubles. Each cabin has it’s own en-suite bathroom and shower, whilst the owners cabin also has a sofa bed and study area. A sixth cabin for VIPs is on the main deck. The pilot berths behind the captain’s office can exceptionally accommodate extra staff or children behind a special partition.

Room to Mingle

Yachting is a very sociable activity, so the redesign ensured there is now plenty of space for entertaining, both inside and on deck. The main communal area is the spacious lounge with an adjoining, fully air‐conditioned games center and dining room. This also has it’s own galley for the quick service of coffee, cocktails, and appetizers.

Dining

When it comes to formal dining, guests can enjoy fine cuisine from the well-equipped galley in one of a number of locations, depending on the time of day, or the weather. The cosy dining room, for example, is fully enclosed, but to enjoy the great outdoors protected from the sun and wind, there is a large dining table under the bimini on the after deck. To dine completely ‘al fresco’ under the stars, there is another table on the open upper deck.

Sunbathing

This upper deck also features a sunbathing and relaxation area of 35m2 with deep cushions, a sofa and comfortable hammocks. In case of strong winds the entire outdoor dining area can be enclosed, whilst still preserving the view.

Tenders and toys info

A cruise isn’t just about what’s on the water; it’s about what’s under, over and beside it too. When redesigning Dione Star to become his main residence, the owner wanted her to carry as many accessories as possible for the full enjoyment of each destination, particularly for keen amateur divers. The new 4.5m hull extension, for example, now provides a diving platform and scuba station, with a compressor and 5 complete diving kits.

Tenders

Helping to shuttle guests to each dive location, and to beaches and ports ashore, is a fleet of three tenders. This includes a 9.7m with a 270hp inboard diesel, a 5.8m with an 115hp outboard, and a 4.5m with a diesel-powered hydrojet, exceptionally safe in crowded waters. In addition, two 98HP Yamaha jetskiis that are housed in two tanks. Cleverly, these tanks can be filled with hot or cold water, either fresh or salt, to double as relaxation tubs.

Floating Platforms

Another surprising accessory is a 24m2 floating carbon platform that straddles inflatable tubes. This can be moored near the yacht, or towed in to shallow water and anchored off a beach. As the yacht also carries a wide range of fishing and snorkelling gear, as well as paddle-boards and inflatable ’towable’ toys, the platform also acts as a base for water sports, or a snack lunch.
