— INSIDE INTRO —

Built in 1991 by a reputable Dutch yard, Dione Star is a classic example of fundamental refinement. She was extensively modernised between 2011-2013 and now boasts every creature comfort you could wish for. Whether your dream is to island hop in the Caribbean, or make a trans-Atlantic passage, Dione Star will carry you in supreme comfort and safety.

The new owner spared no expense during the two-year refit, and worked closely with the original designers to make the yacht even better than new. With the Lloyds-class steel hull lengthened to 43.8m, there is ample accommodation for up to 12 guests, as well as the professional crew of 11.

Dining Room

The dining room has a wooden partition that, when closed, completely separates the dining room from the living room. When the partition opens, both spaces form a single large room, divided by the open bookshelves.
The dining room has two twin tables, thus affording privacy for small parties. At dinnertime, each table can be enlarged to host 10 guests. When one table alone is opened-up, the other one can be used for buffets. During the rest of the day, both are square game tables, with chess marquetry inlaid

Living Room

The living room has a double-layered library, with shelves under the windows that double-up as side tables for a glass or ashtray. You’ll find a vertical Schimmel piano together with a day-bed sitting 4, high enough to afford excellent views of the outside, particularly suitable for sitting inside in cold climates while navigating, and at the same time watching the coastline go bye.

Main Deck Cabin

Of the six double cabins, one is on the main deck, off the dining room. It has an outside door giving straight out onto external walkway and ample windows that fill the cabin with light. The bathroom has a comfortable bathtub, with large showerhead above. When not used, this cabin can turn into a cozy sitting room, one wall fully covered by a pillared library in pale colored wood.

Owner's Cabin

The owner’s cabin can be partitioned to separate the master bed from a small study with a desk and sofa. The sofa can convert into a one-on-two-meters bed. The bathroom has separate toilet and bidet, as well as a bathtub and separate showerhead.

Bathrooms

All 7 bathrooms are fitted in cream-white stone from Carnia, the same stone that frames windows in Venice houses. Below deck is also a guest day toilet you access from the main corridor. It has basin, toilets and a separate shower.
